# Belt Tension Adjustment

## Required Tools
- Belt tension gauge
- Straight edge (1m)
- Tape measure
- Wrench set (10-24mm)
- Pry bar

## Tension Measurement
1. Measure span length (L) between pulley centers
2. Apply force (F) at mid-span until belt deflects
3. Measure deflection (d)
4. Correct deflection = 1.5-2% of L

## Adjustment Steps
1. Loosen motor mounting bolts
2. Adjust take-up screws evenly on both sides
3. Tighten in 1/4 turn increments
4. Check tension after each adjustment
5. Re-tighten motor bolts to torque spec
6. Run for 5 minutes, re-check tension

## Tension Specification
| Belt Type | Deflection | Force |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| V-belt | 1.5% of span | 4-6 kg |
| Timing belt | 2% of span | 6-8 kg |
| Flat belt | 1% of span | 3-5 kg |

## Warning Signs
- Belt slipping → squealing noise, reduced speed
- Too tight → bearing overheating, belt stretching
- Misaligned → uneven wear on one edge
- Glazing → belt has been slipping, replace
