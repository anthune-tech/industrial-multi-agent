# Incident Reporting Procedure

## What to Report
- Safety incidents (near miss, first aid, medical treatment, lost time)
- Equipment damage
- Environmental spills or releases
- Quality incidents (customer complaint, internal non-conformance)
- Security breaches

## Immediate Response (Within 15 Minutes)
1. Ensure area is safe — isolate hazards
2. Provide first aid / call emergency services if needed
3. Preserve the scene — do not disturb evidence
4. Notify supervisor immediately

## Report Submission (Within 1 Hour)
Complete the incident report form with:
- Date, time, and exact location
- Personnel involved (names, roles)
- Description of what happened (facts only, no opinions)
- Equipment involved (machine ID, part numbers)
- Immediate actions taken
- Witness statements (if any)
- Photographs of the scene

## Investigation (Within 24 Hours)
- Supervisor leads initial investigation
- Root cause analysis using 5-Why method
- Identify corrective and preventive actions
- Assign action owners and due dates

## Classification
| Severity | Definition | Response |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Level 1 | Near miss, no injury/damage | Local investigation |
| Level 2 | First aid, minor damage | Department investigation |
| Level 3 | Medical treatment, significant damage | Plant-wide investigation |
| Level 4 | Lost time injury, major damage | Corporate investigation |

## Follow-Up
- Track corrective actions to closure
- Share lessons learned at shift briefs
- Update SOPs if procedure gaps identified
- Review trend metrics monthly
